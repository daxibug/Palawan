How to get to Palawan

Palawan, Philippines, offers world-renowned white-sand beaches, crystal-clear lagoons, and dramatic limestone cliffs. Key spots include El Nido's Hidden Beach and Nacpan Beach, Coron's boat-accessible shores, and the secluded beaches of Balabac, all perfect for island hopping, snorkeling, and diving.

Malcapuya Island

Located off the southern coast of Coron town in Palawan Island, Malcapuya Island is often hailed as one of the most beautiful beaches in the region.

CYC Beach

CYC Beach, short for Coron Youth Club Beach, is one of the most accessible and family-friendly beaches in Coron Town, Palawan Island. Fringed by white sand, crystal-clear waters, and lush mangroves, the beach features a gently sloping shoreline that makes it ideal for kids and casual swimmers.

Banol Beach

Banul Beach is situated in Coron, Palawan. The waters are brilliantly blue, the beaches are perfectly white, and the sheer cliffs will greet you with open views. Go sunbathing on the gorgeous white beaches. Aside from swimming in the nearby reef garden, you can stretch your nature photography muscles.

Long Beach

The longest white sand beach in the Philippines, stretching approximately 14.7 kilometers. Located in San Vicente, it is known for its cream-colored sand, shallow turquoise waters, and as an emerging sustainable tourism destination that is roughly three times longer than Boracay’s White sand beach.

Maxima Beach

Maxima Island lies off the coast of Port Barton beach village in San Vicente town, Palawan, and is one of the less trodden jewels in the province’s archipelago of islets.

Alimanguan Beach

Tucked away on the stunning coastline, Alimanguan beach is a tranquil paradise characterized by pristine sands that stretch out into the distance and clear waters that effortlessly blend with the horizon.

Nagtabon Beach

Nagtabon Beach offers an unparalleled experience with its pristine golden sands and azure waters, framed by lush greenery and towering coconut palms.

Coconut Beach

Coconut Beach is one of the most picturesque spots in Port Barton, boasting a pristine shoreline fringed with swaying coconut palms and crystal-clear waters. It’s a tropical paradise perfect for relaxing and soaking in the beauty of nature.
