How to get to Palawan

Palawan island hopping centers on El Nido's Bacuit Archipelago (dramatic lagoons/limestone cliffs) and Coron (shipwrecks/lakes), offering world-class snorkeling, white sand beaches, and boat tours (A-D).

Big Lagoon (Tour A)

Famous for emerald waters surrounded by towering limestone cliffs, best explored by kayak.

Small Lagoon (Tour A/D)

A hidden, tranquil lagoon accessed through a small crevice in the rocks.

Secret Beach (Tour C)

A hidden beach tucked behind limestone walls, accessed by swimming through a narrow tunnel.

Shimizu Island (Tour A/C)

A popular spot for snorkeling, known for its vibrant coral reefs and marine life.

Snake Island (Tour B)

Features a unique, long S-shaped sandbar connecting the island to the mainland.

Helicopter Island (Tour C)

Named for its distinct shape, offering white sand beaches.

Cadlao Lagoon (Tour D)

A tranquil, often less crowded lagoon on Cadlao Island.

Kayangan Lake

Widely considered the cleanest lake in Asia, Kayangan is the most iconic spot in Coron.

Twin Lagoon

This destination consists of two stunning lagoons separated by a giant limestone wall.

Barracuda Lake

Barracuda Lake is famous for its surreal underwater rock formations that look like a mountain range beneath the surface.

Skeleton Wreck

This is one of the most accessible WWII shipwrecks in Coron. The "skeleton" of a 25-meter-long Japanese supply ship rests at a shallow depth (about 5 to 22 meters).

Malcapuya Island

If you are looking for the classic "tropical paradise" beach, Malcapuya is the top choice. Located a bit further from Coron town (usually part of Tour C), it features a long, pristine stretch of powdery white sand and shallow turquoise waters.

Best Way to Explore

  • Tour A: Best for lagoons and kayaking
  • Tour C: Best for snorkeling and scenic beaches
  • Tour B & D: Better for relaxing, caves, and avoiding the heaviest crowds.
