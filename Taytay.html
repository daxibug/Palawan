How to get to Palawan

Taytay, known as the “Estrella Del Norte” or “The Star of the North,” is a municipality located on the northern part of Palawan Island in the Philippines. With its strategic location facing both the South China Sea and Sulu Sea, Taytay boasts beautiful islands with clear blue waters that make it a popular destination for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts. Additionally, as one of the oldest towns in the province and the first capital, Taytay is steeped in history and culture, offering visitors a glimpse into its rich heritage.

Beaches

Pabellon Islands

Pabellon Islands, also known as Elephant Island, boasts of magnificent rockscapes surrounded by white sand beaches and a large coral community. The island also conceals a natural, hidden lagoon tucked deep within the cliffs. This lagoon is home to a species of bird known as "Balinsasayaw", who make birds nests which are harvested and considered a delicacy to the native population. During the gathering season, visitors can watch the natives of the Island climb the jagged rocks and harvest the nests, then enjoy the delicious native cuisine.

Activities to do

  • Sea Kayaking & Island Hopping
  • Snorkeling & Diving
  • Relax on White Sand Beach
  • Photography & Birdwatching

Isla Blanca

Isla Blanca is a spot features pristine white sandy beaches and crystal-clear waters, attracting those seeking tranquility and a connection with nature. The area is not heavily commercialized, providing an authentic experience of the local environment. Visitors can enjoy various water activities such as swimming and snorkeling, as well as explore nearby islands and marine life. Its serene atmosphere makes it an ideal escape for travelers looking to unwind.

Activities to do

  • Kiteboarding & Relaxing at Isla Blanca Beach
  • Exploring Chamucuc Lagoon
  • Fishing & Boat Tours
  • ATV Adventures
  • Camping & Beach Picnics

Flower Island

Flower Island is a stunning rustic retreat with 20 bungalows nestled amidst lush tropical vegetation, pristine white sand beaches, and crystal-clear waters brimming with marine life. This secluded island sanctuary is the perfect destination for those seeking a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. As one of the most breathtaking islands in Taytay, Flower Island Resort offers a romantic haven for couples looking to unwind and celebrate their love in seclusion. With only 15 villas scattered across the island’s beachfront, guests can bask in the gentle sea breeze and the soothing sound of waves lapping the shore.

Activities to do

  • Pearl Farm Tour
  • Snorkeling & Diving
  • Water Sports
  • Nature Walks & Hiking
  • Relaxation & Wellness
  • Island Activities

Nabat Island

Nabat Island is a must-visit destination for snorkeling enthusiasts, offering rich coral reefs and a diverse array of tropical fish. The island is also home to caves that are inhabited by Balinsasayaw birds, which build their nests with their own solidified saliva. Edible bird’s nests are one of the most expensive delicacies in the world and are highly prized, particularly in Asia, for their purported health benefits.

Activities to do

  • Cave Exploration
  • Diving & Snorkeling
  • Island Hopping & Beach Picnic
  • Photography & Nature Viewing
  • Bird Watching

Dinamayan Island

Dinamayan Island is a true gem with its stunning white sand beach and thriving underwater world. A short boat ride from Taytay port, it is privately owned by the same owner of Casa Rosa Pension in Taytay, so arranging your trip to Dinamayan Island through Casa Rosa is easy and convenient.

Activities to do

  • Diving & Snorkeling
  • Island Hopping & Boat Tours
  • Photography & Beach Relaxing
  • Marine Wildlife Watching
